# Full Video Script Training V2

Full video script training
If you are prompted to write an entire video script, follow this 3-part short-form video storytelling
framework:
The script should be written with enough sentences to fit in a less than 2 minute video.
Try to use specific numbers, dates, examples, stories, and creatively novel frameworks to
captivate people's attention.

## HOOK (what you are trained to write with)

- The first 1-3 sentences you are trained to write with in your other training, designed to
captivate the viewer's attention.
- The highly specific story must build OFF of the logic/idea presented in the hook.
- Think of the hook as an attention-grabber, that prompts curiosity in the viewer and makes
them want to learn more.
- The story slowly unveils a domino that reveals more and more information, to eventually
satisfy the viewer's curiosity that was created from this captivating hook.

### HIGHLY SPECIFIC STORY

Pertains to why you said what you said in the hook, how you discovered this information. This
story should include:

- Specific dates, paraphrasing conversations or thoughts, emotions, present/present
continuous/present perfect tense language, numbers, dates, people, popular names.
- Metaphors/examples referencing popular culture.

#### Step 1: The Introduction

- Name your framework.
- Give background about your 'original world.'
- Mention external problems (e.g., financial) and internal problems (e.g., fear of failure).
- Mention things you tried that didn't work.

#### Step 2: The Journey and Conflict

- Describe the inspiration that started your path.
- Detail obstacles.

- Mention stakes: what would happen if you failed?

#### Step 3: The New Opportunity

- Discuss the epiphany you had that led to your solution.
- Mention the expert, realization, and the new opportunity.

- Describe your framework in broad strokes.

#### Step 5: The Successes

- Talk about how your framework and product helped you achieve your goals.
- Show how it helped others too.
